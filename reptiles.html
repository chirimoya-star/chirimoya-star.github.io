Reptiles

Read time: 5 minutes

Reptiles are cold-blooded animals with scaly skin, known for their incredible adaptability and ancient lineage. From the smallest geckos to giant crocodiles, reptiles have thrived on Earth for over 300 million years, making them some of the planet’s oldest creatures.

-

Some Endangered Reptile Species:

Komodo Dragon (Varanus komodoensis):

o Description: The Komodo dragon is the largest living lizard, growing up to 10 feet in length. These powerful reptiles are known for their venomous bite and incredible hunting skills.

o Habitat: Dry, open grasslands and savannas of Indonesia’s Komodo Island.

o Diet: Carnivorous – hunts large prey like deer, wild boar, and even water buffalo.

o Conservation Status: Vulnerable due to habitat destruction and human interference.

-

Green Sea Turtle (Chelonia mydas):

o Description: The green sea turtle is a large, herbivorous reptile found in tropical oceans worldwide. Known for its graceful swimming, it returns to the same beach where it was born to lay its eggs.

o Habitat: Warm coastal waters and seagrass meadows.

o Diet: Herbivorous – primarily feeds on seagrass and algae.

o Conservation Status: Endangered due to pollution, illegal poaching, and climate change.
