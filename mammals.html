Mammals

Read time: 5 minutes

Mammals are a diverse group of warm-blooded animals known for their hair or fur and the ability to nurse their young with milk. With over 5,000 species, mammals can be found in almost every habitat on Earth, from the deep oceans to the highest mountains.

-

Some Vulnerable Mammal Species:

African Elephant (Loxodonta africana):

• Description: The African elephant is the largest land animal on Earth, known for its massive ears, long trunk, and strong social bonds. These gentle giants can live up to 70 years in the wild.

• Habitat: Savannas, forests, and deserts of sub-Saharan Africa.

• Diet: Herbivorous – feeding on grasses, fruits, and bark.

• Conservation Status: Vulnerable due to poaching and habitat destruction.

-

Bengal Tiger (Panthera tigris tigris):

• Description: This iconic predator is known for its striking orange coat with black stripes. Bengal tigers are solitary hunters and are vital for maintaining the balance of their ecosystems.

• Habitat: Tropical forests, grasslands, and mangroves of South Asia.

• Diet: Carnivorous – primarily feeds on large mammals such as deer and wild boar.

• Conservation Status: Endangered due to poaching and habitat loss.
